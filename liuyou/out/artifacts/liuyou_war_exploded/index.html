Liu Gong Dao

Liu Gong Island is located at the eastern end of the Shandong Peninsula in eastern China, at the mouth of Weihai Bay. The island covers an area of 3.15 square kilometers, with a coastline of 14.95 kilometers. It serves as a natural maritime barrier for Weihai City and holds a crucial strategic position in national defense, earning it the titles of "Eastern Frontier Barrier" and the "Unsinkable Warship." Numerous maritime conflicts occurred here from the Ming and Qing dynasties to before the Liberation, with the First Sino-Japanese War, in particular, leaving a profound historical imprint.

ChengShan Cape

Chengshan Head, also known as "Tian Jin Tou," is located in Chengshan Town, Rongcheng City, Shandong Province, China. It is named for being at the easternmost end of the Chengshan Mountain Range. Chengshan Head is surrounded by the sea on three sides and connected to the land on one side, only 5 nautical miles from the south-north international main shipping channel and 94 nautical miles away from South Korea across the sea.

Xianguting Scenic Area

owering peaks, rugged and peculiar rocks, towering trees, and a profusion of exotic flowers and plants cover the area. Serene and secluded, ancient and robust, the scenery is enchanting. Reaching the summit brings a sense of tranquility and joy, with a panoramic view to the east of the sea and Liu Gong Island, the cityscape visible to the north, and the lush green mountains and Bohai Bay to the west. This natural paradise, rich in beauty, combined with mystical legends, creates a captivating destination.

Sea Donkey Island

Looking across the sea, the island reveals steep cliffs adorned with colorful flowers, and flocks of seagulls hover and circle above. The entire island resembles a slender donkey lying in the sea, hence the name "Hailu Island." Due to the abundance of seagulls on the island, and the local term for seagulls being "haimaozi" (sea cats), Hailu Island is also known as Haimao Island.
